The labor “Robocalypse” is here...

Editor's Note: Today, we depart from our normal Saturday fare to share key insight from Tom. Last month, Tom revealed the reason for the current stock market decline. It's a global economic shift he calls "The Great Unwinding." Below, he tells you where the effects of The Great Unwinding are worst, and what to do to protect yourself... and profit.

A 100%-autonomous aerial drone just made its first delivery...

The Associated Press reports it flew a half-mile to drop off a package in a residential area in Reno, Nevada.

The drone joins a large and growing list of robots now doing the work of humans around the globe...

  • Domino’s Pizza is testing an autonomous pizza delivery robot in Australia. The vehicle can travel 12 miles per trip and drive up to 12 miles per hour.
  • At two of the busiest U.S. ports, giant red autonomous robots now manage cargo transfer and storage. They’ll cut the need for longshore labor by 50%.
  • At mines worldwide, gigantic autonomous dump trucks now haul 250 tons per load—safely.

Perhaps the CEO of burger chain Carl’s Jr. summed up automation’s disruptive labor market trend the best:

If you’re making labor more expensive [by hiking the minimum wage], and automation less expensive—this is not rocket science.

He hopes to create a restaurant chain where “you never see a person.”

Bottom line: Up to 47% of existing U.S. jobs may die out over the ensuing decade.

Check now to see if your own occupation is at risk. Then, follow Mark’s advice: Start developing additional side income streams now... before the robots take your job.

Earn a lifetime of wealth on just four “quarters”...

From Mark Ford, founder, Palm Beach Research Group: I’ve adopted a new metaphor for my life... a metaphor I’ve found very useful.

At birth, we’re given four quarters. The first is childhood. The second is young adulthood. The third is maturity. And the last is the great hurrah.

If we are careful and lucky, we get to spend all four quarters.

What we do with those quarters—whether we waste them or spend them wisely—is up to us.

By any measure of life expectancy, I’m spending my fourth quarter now. When I look back at how I spent my first three quarters, I can see that although I threw lots of pennies away, I also invested lots of pennies wisely.

Overall, I got a very good return on the pennies of time I was given.

But here I am, spending that last quarter, and I’m still throwing pennies away. It’s not that I don’t know what my priorities are. I’ve imagined my funeral, and I know what my core values are. I can no longer put off these important-but-not-urgent goals.

If you’re in your fourth quarter, I’m writing this to tell you time’s a wasting. It’s now or never. If you’re younger, why not get on board now? You’ll have fewer regrets and a richer life. What’s wrong with that?
